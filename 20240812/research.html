Our group research has been focusing on theoretical and computational heterogeneous catalysis.

We take the theoretical and computational chemistry approach to tackle reaction mechanisms, structure-performance relationship, and ration design in heterogeneous catalysis, with particular focuses on activation and conversion of small inert molecules (CO2, H2O, N2, alkanes etc.) by electrocatalysis, single-atom and single-cluster catalysis.

So far, we have made contributions on:

  • Potential-dependent kinetics and mechanisms in heterogeneous electrocatalysis (we also develop new methods for modeling catalysis at electrochemical interfaces);
  • Structure-performance relationships in single-atom and single-cluster catalysis;
  • Rational design in heterogeneous catalysis (including novel design concepts).

Please check our Publications along these research lines.

In addition, we have been working on (with publications yet to come):

  • New methods for more accurate predictions in heterogeneous catalysis;
  • Data-driven paradigms for chemical theory and computation.
